Laptop Requirements

Students need a Windows laptop that is suitable for daily school use and supports an active stylus or digital pen.

What type of laptop should I buy?

The laptop should be light enough to carry each day, powerful enough for schoolwork, and have enough battery life to last through the school day.

Touchscreen is not the same as active stylus support. Look for wording such as active stylus support, active pen support, digitiser, pen included, Microsoft Pen Protocol (MPP), or Wacom AES.

Recommended style

A 13 to 14 inch convertible or 2-in-1 Windows laptop is usually the best option for school use. It should have a proper keyboard, a screen suitable for reading and writing, and a tablet-style mode for handwriting and annotation.

Buying advice

Parents may buy from any trusted UK retailer, provided the laptop meets the school requirements. A brand-new laptop is strongly recommended because it is more likely to have good battery life, warranty cover and a longer useful lifespan.

A refurbished or used laptop may be accepted if it meets the requirements, but parents should check that the battery is in good condition, the device supports Windows 11 and active stylus input, the charger is included, and the device is not locked to another organisation.
